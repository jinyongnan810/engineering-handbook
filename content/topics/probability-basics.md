# Probability basics

Probability measures how likely something is to happen.

$$
0 \le P(A) \le 1
$$

## Basic probability rule

$$
P(A) = \frac{\text{number of favorable outcomes}}{\text{number of total outcomes}}
$$

## Complement probability

The complement means “not A.”

$$
P(A^c) = 1 - P(A)
$$

## Joint probability

Joint probability means the probability that two things both happen.

$$
P(A \cap B)
$$

## Independent events

Two events are independent if one event does not affect the probability of the other.
For independent events:

$$
P(A \cap B) = P(A)P(B)
$$

## Union probability

Union probability means the probability that at least one of two things happens.

$$
P(A \cup B) = P(A) + P(B) - P(A \cap B)
$$

## Dependent events

Two events are dependent if the first event changes the probability of the second event.

## Summary

| Concept            | Meaning              | Formula                                      |
| ------------------ | -------------------- | -------------------------------------------- |
| Basic probability  | favorable / total    | $P(A)=\frac{\text{favorable}}{\text{total}}$ |
| Complement         | probability of not A | $P(A^c)=1-P(A)$                              |
| Joint probability  | A and B happen       | $P(A \cap B)$                                |
| Independent events | A does not affect B  | $P(A \cap B)=P(A)P(B)$                       |
| Union              | A or B happens       | $P(A \cup B)=P(A)+P(B)-P(A \cap B)$          |
